Ucaima Avitourism Proposal - Source Notes
Access date: 2026-06-08

1. Canaima official / protected-area baseline
- Inparques, "Parque Nacional Canaima"
  URL: https://www.inparques.gob.ve/parque-nacional-canaima/
  Used for: official park context, fauna statement, "mas de 587 especies" of birds, harpy eagle/macaw/hummingbird/toucan examples.
  Relevant page lines from web capture: 81-84.

- UNESCO World Heritage Centre, "Canaima National Park"
  URL: https://whc.unesco.org/en/list/701/
  Used for: 3,000,000 ha property size, 65% tepui formations, World Heritage context, 1994 inscription.
  Relevant page lines from web capture: 331-342 and 418-423.

2. Bird checklist / target species
- Avibase, "Canaima National Park bird checklist"
  URL: https://avibase.bsc-eoc.org/checklist.jsp?region=VEbo01
  Used for: 736 species, 5 globally threatened species, last modified 2026-02-17, and examples of target species in the checklist.
  Relevant page lines from web capture: 92-99.
  Example species lines:
  - Tepui Tinamou, country/region endemic: 113-117.
  - Tepui Goldenthroat, country/region endemic: 227-230.
  - Roraiman Barbtail and Tepui Spinetail: 632-634.
  - Guianan Cock-of-the-rock and White Bellbird: 657-668.
  Caveat: the deck does not claim these birds are only in Canaima. It uses the safer phrasing "tepui and Guianan Shield specialties" and "route list to be field validated."

3. North America demand proof
- U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service, 2022 National Survey of Fishing, Hunting, and Wildlife-Associated Recreation
  URL: https://www.fws.gov/sites/default/files/documents/Final_2022-National-Survey_101223-accessible-single-page.pdf
  Used for: 96.3M U.S. wild bird observers, 42.6M taking away-from-home trips to observe wild birds, 148.3M wildlife watchers, $250.2B wildlife-watching expenditures.
  Local PDF extract used page 30 text and expenditure table text.

- U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service, "Birdwatching in America"
  URL: https://www.fws.gov/story/2024-12/birdwatching-america
  Used for: birding participation and $107.6B U.S. birding spend summary.

4. Colombia benchmark
- Colombia Travel, "Birdwatching"
  URL: https://colombia.travel/en/birdwatching
  Used for: 1,900+ bird species, 79 endemic species, nearly 170 migratory species, and official tourism positioning that birdwatching can happen any month of the year.
  Relevant page lines from web capture: 55-68.

- Ministerio de Comercio, Industria y Turismo de Colombia, "Colombia es el mejor pais para pajarear..."
  URL: https://www.mincit.gov.co/prensa/noticias/turismo/primer-lugar-colombia-en-el-global-big-day-2024
  Used for: Global Big Day 2024 result, 1,558 species and 12,007 checklists, Colombia positioning as birdwatching leader, and government support for routes/equipment/capacity building.
  Relevant page lines from web capture: 91-121.

- Presidencia de Colombia, "Colombia rompio nuevo record..."
  URL: https://www.presidencia.gov.co/prensa/Paginas/Colombia-rompio-nuevo-record-en-turismo-al-recibir-a-cerca-de-67-millones-de-visitantes-no-residentes-en-2024-250121.aspx
  Used for: 2024 non-resident visitor record and U.S. participation as 26.5% of visitors from that country.
  Search result captured: "cerca de 6,7 millones" and 1,194,642 visitors from the United States, 26.5%.

5. Seasonality support
- Colombia Travel, "Birdwatching"
  URL: https://colombia.travel/en/birdwatching
  Used for: proof that a birdwatching product can be positioned as year-round when routes and specialized guides are available.

- MondoParks, "Canaima National Park: The Lost World"
  URL: https://mondoparks.com/parks/canaima/
  Used only as operational context for dry/rainy season product logic: rainy season June-November, dry season December-April.
  Caveat: seasonality must be validated with Ucaima/local operators before publication.
